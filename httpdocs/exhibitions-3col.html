CURRENT EXHIBITIONS

SURVIVOR: DADANG CHRISTANTO

21 JULY - 26 AUGUST, GALLERY 3 AND 24:7

Survivor is a major art project by contemporary Indonesian-Australian artist Dadang Christanto. Initially developed in 2008 as a live protest against an environmental disaster that occurred in East Java which saw hot volcanic mud wipe out 11 villages and destroy many lives, Survivor has since transgressed beyond cultural borders, evolving into a universal, multi-faceted reflection upon human survival and what it means to endure...

Read more about the exhibition

BANANAS, BUSINESS AND BOCCE:
THE LISMORE ITALIANS

21 JULY - 26 AUGUST, GALLERY 1 AND 2

An Interpretation by Leonie Lane and the Lismore Region's Italian Community, Bananas, Business and Bocce: The Lismore Italians tells the stories of people who journeyed from Italy to Lismore to make a new life for themselves and their families. It reflects the heritage of the Italian community in Lismore and draw from historical collections, photographs and personal memories.

Read more about the exhibition

BLISS: FIONA FOLEY

21 JULY - 26 AUGUST, UPSTAIRS GALLERY

Bliss explores the hidden history of settler and Indigenous relations in Queensland, and in particular, the 1897 Aboriginals Protection and Restriction of the Sale of Opium Act. Foley points to the role this Act played in the subjugation of Indigenous people in Queensland, through creating a dependency on narcotics, as well contributing to troubled relations between White, Asian and Indigenous peoples.

Read more about the exhibition
